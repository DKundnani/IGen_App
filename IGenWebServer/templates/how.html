How It Works?

All humans share over 99% of our DNA. The 1% that differs is what makes us unique. iGen analyzes this unique data, called variants, to discover your susceptibility to certain diseases.


We analyze your data based on results from recently published, peer-reviewed genome-wide association (GWAS) studies. GWAS studies scan the genomes of thousands of people to find variants that are associated with certain traits, such as disease susceptibility. We use the variants found in these studies and compare it with your DNA to see if your genome has any of the variants associated with a disease.

Every variant in the genome has a different contribution to a given trait and the studies weigh the amount of contribution. We use these weights with your genomic information to calculate your susceptibility.


In order to keep results informative, we provide a percentile score to users, indicating their risk for susceptibility or disease progression, when compared to how thousands of other people are performing.
